The Spirit of Sindhiyat

The Sindhi civilization is one of the oldest in the world, tracing its roots to the Indus Valley Civilization that flourished over 5,000 years ago along the banks of the Indus River (Sindhu). The word "Hindu" itself is derived from "Sindhu," reflecting the deep historical significance of the Sindhi people.

Sindhis are known for their resilience, entrepreneurship, and warm hospitality. Despite being displaced from their homeland during the 1947 partition, Sindhis have thrived across the globe while preserving their unique cultural identity — their Sindhiyat.

Our culture is a beautiful tapestry woven with vibrant festivals, delicious cuisine, a rich literary tradition, soulful music, and an unwavering spirit of community and togetherness.

Our Festivals

Vibrant celebrations that keep our traditions alive and bring the community together

🌊

Cheti Chand

The Sindhi New Year, celebrating the birth of Uderolal (Jhulelal), the patron saint and Ishta Dev of Sindhis. Observed on the first day of the Hindu month of Chaitra, it is the most important festival for the Sindhi community. Celebrations include Jhulelal Puja, Bahrana Sahib processions, and community feasts.

🪔

Diwali

The Festival of Lights is celebrated with great fervor by Sindhis. Homes are decorated with diyas and rangoli, Lakshmi Puja is performed for prosperity, and families gather for grand celebrations with sweets, crackers, and cultural programs. A time of joy, renewal, and togetherness.

🎨

Holi

The Festival of Colors celebrates the arrival of spring and the triumph of good over evil. Sindhis celebrate with great enthusiasm, playing with colors, singing folk songs, preparing special delicacies like gujiya, and sharing joy with the community.

🍃

Teejri

A cherished Sindhi festival observed by married women for their husbands' well-being. Celebrated in the month of Shravan with fasting, mehndi, the Teejri Katha, and the sacred moonrise Arghya ritual offering rice, milk, and cucumber to the moon.

🕉️

Thanksgiving (Thakur Dino)

The Sindhi day of thanksgiving dedicated to giving gratitude to God (Thakur) for blessings received. Families prepare special meals, visit temples, and spend time in prayer and reflection, expressing gratitude for all they have.

🎉

Lal Loi

A bonfire festival celebrated by Sindhis on the eve of Makar Sankranti. Families gather around bonfires, sing traditional songs, share til (sesame) sweets, and welcome the harvest season with joy and community spirit.

The Sindhi Language

Sindhi is an ancient Indo-Aryan language with a rich literary heritage spanning over a thousand years. It is written in a modified Arabic script in Pakistan and in Devanagari script in India.

The Sindhi language is known for its unique sounds and extensive vocabulary. It has a beautiful literary tradition, with poets like Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai (whose masterwork "Shah Jo Risalo" is considered one of the great works of world literature), Sachal Sarmast, and Sami being revered for their spiritual and philosophical poetry.

At SAS, we encourage the preservation of the Sindhi language through community events, storytelling sessions, and cultural programs. We believe that language is the foundation of cultural identity, and we strive to pass this heritage to our children.

Sindhi Cuisine

A delicious journey through the flavors that define Sindhi culinary tradition

🫓

Dal Pakwan

The quintessential Sindhi breakfast — crispy fried flatbread served with spiced chana dal, a beloved Sunday morning tradition.

🥬

Sai Bhaji

A hearty, nutritious dish of mixed greens, lentils, and vegetables — the comfort food of every Sindhi household.

🍛

Sindhi Biryani

A spicy, flavorful rice dish with potatoes, meat, and aromatic spices — a festive favorite at every celebration.

🥘

Sindhi Kadhi

A tangy, chickpea flour-based curry loaded with vegetables — the everyday staple that every Sindhi craves.

🧆

Koki

A crispy, spiced flatbread made with onions, green chilies, and fresh herbs — perfect for breakfast or snack time.

🫕

Seyal Mani

Bread cooked in a spicy tomato-onion gravy — a simple yet delicious leftover transformation loved by all.

🍮

Mithai

Traditional Sindhi sweets like Tosha, Singhar ji Mithai, and Ladoo that sweeten every celebration and festival.

🥟

Papad jo Saag

A unique Sindhi curry made with papad pieces in a spicy, tangy gravy — a signature comfort dish.

Music, Arts & Traditions

The artistic soul of the Sindhi people

🎵

Folk Music

Rich tradition of folk songs, bhajans, and Sufi music. The legacy of Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai lives on through soulful melodies.

💃

Dance

Traditional Sindhi folk dances like Jhumar, Ho Jamalo, and Dhamaal are performed at weddings and festivals with great energy.

🎭

Ajrak & Topi

The iconic Sindhi Ajrak (block-printed shawl) and Sindhi Topi (cap) are symbols of Sindhi identity and cultural pride.

📚

Literature

A rich literary tradition spanning centuries, from the poetry of Shah Latif to modern Sindhi writers preserving our stories.
